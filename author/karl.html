cabinet

In our garage we have a wall cabinet, wich is actually put together from two parts and has three doors. It is unpractical because long items cannot be put into it. I wanted to replace it with another cabinet, not a shelf, because I use my tablesaw in the garage among other things and it would be nice to have things protected from the dust.

This is the old cabinet:

.. and taken off the wall: It was not fastened properly. All those long thick nails only went into the drywall - they missed the studs completely.

My cabinet should be a single cabinet which is bigger than the old double unit. Cabinets of that size cannot be bought as comodity. But I thought it should be possible to make one.

I use mortise and tenon joints to join front and back.

Using the router to carve the mortise:

This is how the front/back joints look:

I use finger-joints for the front and back frames, mainly because I can make them quite well now ( The old cabinet frame was joined with pocket-screws ).

The fingerjoints for the face - fits well ( luckily ):

Gluing the front onto the back while inserting the side panels. The fit was not optimal and I could not get it completely closed ( but the gap is visible only when you look closely ). I wonder how professionals always seem to get these things to work so well ?

Painted:

I make the doors just like to front and back faces; but I only used one 2x4 for each that I then ripped into two equal halves.

The parts for the doors are glued up:

Here I rip the frame in half, because it is supposed to be for both doors:

The backs are glued on:

and painted:

I mounted the doors with normal door-hinges; first a try-fit:

The cabinet on the wall: For this I used an angle iron that I had lying around; I screwed it to the wall and then lifted the cabinet over it.

One shelf is missing, but I don't have any board that is wide enough at hand. This is my first ever attempt at gluing two boards together lenghtwise. I used dowels for the alignment.

ready for the glue-up:

Almost finished. I'm gluing in magnets, so that the door will close with a satisfying 'clack':

To consider how heavy all of this has become, I weighed the parts.

Weight in lbs new old
angle iron 1 -
doors 15 (2) 30 (3)
frame 35 20 + 25
boards 25 14
total 75 89

Conclusion: The frame is overly thick. If I ever did make another similar cabinet, I would make the front and back frames with half the thickness, as that should be adequate.

Kinderlieder

Kinderlieder

Eine Sprache lernen, das tut man mithilfe von Sprache, Spass soll es machen, und einprägsam sein. Keiner lernt wirklich durch stures Vokabelpauken; selbst wenn man die Disziplin für so etwas hat, muss doch ein "Aha"-Moment dazukommen.

Zusammen Lieder zu singen bietet Spass und Geselligkeit und es ist damit auch um ein vieles leichter, neues zu vermitteln.

Im Internet wimmelt es vor Youtube-Videos von Kinderliedern. und Bezahl-portalen, die eine eher schlechte als rechte Vorschau bereithalten.

Seit es Lilypond gibt, gibt es auch freie Musik Mutopia. Warum nicht auch die Kinderlieder, die ich grade singen will ?

Zum Copyright: CC-BY-SA-4.0 . Kopiere, wie Du willst, aber sage, woher es kam !

Auf einer Konferenz sass ich einmal in einem Vortrag, dessen Thema mir sehr vertraut war, der Vortragende ein ehemaliger Kollege. Er hatte an meiner Arbeit ein kleines bischen verändert, und war ganz stolz, das ganze zu präsentieren. Meinen Namen zu erwähnen hielt er nicht für nötig.

Jun 06, 2016

bunkbed

The idea was that Erwin and Lukas sleep in one room, do not disturb each other, and the bed doesn't take too much area. For the sides I used pieces I already had ( because I had built a single bed for Lukas before ).

I approximately copied the basic plan from Markus Wandel, hier is Markus' version: http://www.woodgears.ca/bed/bunk_bed/built.html.

I had to build the headboards from scratch, and I didn't work as precisely as I had hoped. In the end, it turned out OK, there are no gaps visible ( there were some in the first bed, so this is an improvement ).

Wood tends to get dirty when it is not varnished, or rather, the dirt cannot be wiped off easily, so I varnished everything, outside.

The mattresses are still missing, but Erwin wanted to explore.
